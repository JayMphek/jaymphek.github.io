Research Overview

This Honours research project addresses the critical need for early breast cancer detection in South Africa, where healthcare resources are often limited and access to specialized radiologists is constrained. By leveraging artificial intelligence, this system aims to assist healthcare professionals in making more accurate and timely diagnoses.

Research Context & Motivation

Breast cancer is one of the leading causes of cancer-related deaths among women globally, and early detection significantly improves treatment outcomes and survival rates. This research focuses on developing an AI-powered system that can autonomously detect and classify breast cancer abnormalities in mammography images, providing crucial support to healthcare professionals in resource-constrained environments.

System Architecture & Innovation

The novelty of this system lies in its dual-phased architecture:

  • Detection Stage: Autonomously locates potential abnormalities in mammogram images without prior annotation
  • Classification Stage: Predicts the type of abnormality, assesses severity (benign or malignant), and estimates size in pixels

The system visually highlights detected abnormalities using bounding shapes, providing clear visual feedback to healthcare professionals for enhanced diagnostic accuracy.

Methodology & Approach

The research employs Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) as the primary deep learning architecture for both detection and classification tasks. The system processes full-field mammogram images through multiple stages:

  1. Image Preprocessing: Standardization and enhancement of mammogram images
  2. Feature Extraction: CNN layers automatically learn relevant features from mammogram data
  3. Abnormality Detection: Identification and localization of suspicious regions
  4. Classification: Categorization of detected abnormalities by type and severity
  5. Visualization: Generation of bounding boxes and diagnostic reports

Dataset & Training

The system is trained and evaluated using the DMID Breast Cancer Mammography Dataset, which includes:

  • Full-field mammogram images from diverse patient populations
  • Clinical annotations including tissue type classifications
  • BI-RADS (Breast Imaging Reporting and Data System) scores
  • Abnormality coordinates and detailed medical metadata
  • Ground truth labels for both detection and classification tasks

This comprehensive dataset enables the system to learn from real clinical scenarios and develop robust diagnostic capabilities.

Expected Outcomes & Impact

Upon completion, this research aims to deliver:

  • High Accuracy: A system capable of detecting breast cancer abnormalities with radiologist-level precision
  • Clinical Utility: A tool that can be integrated into existing healthcare workflows to assist medical professionals
  • Accessibility: Technology that can help bridge the gap in specialized healthcare access, particularly in underserved regions
  • Research Contribution: Novel architectural approaches to dual-phase medical image analysis

Potential Applications

Beyond breast cancer detection, the methodologies developed in this research could be adapted for:

  • Other forms of medical imaging analysis (CT scans, MRI, X-rays)
  • Telemedicine and remote diagnostic applications
  • Medical education and training tools
  • Population-level screening programs
