  • Research topic: AI-Driven Mammography Detection and Classification for Breast Cancer Diagnosis in South Africa

    This Honours research project proposes the development of an AI-driven mammography analysis system designed to assist in the early detection and classification of breast cancer. The system leverages deep learning techniques‚Äîspecifically Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs)‚Äîto automatically process full-field mammogram images and identify abnormal regions without prior annotation. The novelty of the system lies in its dual-phased architecture: a detection stage that autonomously locates potential abnormalities and visually highlights them using bounding shapes, followed by a classification stage that predicts the type of abnormality, assesses its severity (benign or malignant), and estimates its size in pixels. The system will be trained and evaluated using the DMID Breast Cancer Mammography Dataset, which includes not only image data but also clinical annotations like tissue type, BI-RADS scores, and abnormality coordinates. These features allow the system to simulate radiologist-like interpretations and support clinical decision-making.
